## BOOK-SPECIFIC WORLD-BUILDING

### Primary Locations (Expanded)

#### The Ribcage District

**Physical Description**:
- Towering bone arches rise 200 feet, visible from anywhere in the city
- Ribs curve overhead like cathedral vaulting
- Gaps between ribs create natural "streets" and defensive positions
- Bone-dust perpetually drifts down like snow
- Echo distortion—sounds bounce strangely off curved bone

**Population & Culture**:
- **Mixed district**: Vigil wardens, reformed monsters, mortal families
- **Class stratification**: Upper ribs (wealthy, safer), lower ribs (poor, dangerous)
- **Night markets**: Illegal but tolerated, Grace dens hidden in marrow-pockets
- **Patrol culture**: Vigil members seen as both protectors and occupiers

**Sensory Details**:
- **Sight**: Lamplight filtered through translucent bone (warm amber glow)
- **Sound**: Constant creaking as bones settle, wind whistling through gaps
- **Smell**: Incense from bone-shrines mixing with decay
- **Touch**: Bone surfaces are cold, slightly porous, absorb moisture

**Key Sub-Locations**:
1. **Blood Cisterns**: Pools of the god's congealed heart-blood, used for rituals and medicine
2. **Bone Markets**: Carved marrow-stalls selling relics, charms, forbidden contracts
3. **The Hanging Gardens**: Plants grown in marrow-soil between ribs, maintained by healers

#### The Heart-Chamber

**Physical Description**:
- Massive preserved heart still pulsing faintly with divine energy (3-second intervals)
- Chamber is humid, blood-warm (body temperature)
- Arteries form natural archways into ritual sub-chambers
- Walls pulse visibly with residual godpower
- Floor is slick, requires careful footing

**Function**:
- **Temple Complex**: Priest quarters, ritual chambers, sanctified spaces
- **Prime Contract Heart-Node**: Where the entity feeds on contracts signed here
- **Power Amplification**: Magic performed here is 10x stronger (and more dangerous)

**Atmosphere**:
- Oppressive, sacred/profane energy
- Hard to breathe (thick with divine residue)
- Whispers even when silent (the heart remembers prayers)
- Time feels distorted (seconds stretch, minutes compress)

**Access**:
- Restricted to priests, Contract Lords, elite Vigil members
- Requires blood-key (drops of blood to enter specific chambers)
- Lysa gains access via Tribunal authority
- Cyran has access as former temple ornament

#### The Vein Markets

**Physical Description**:
- Network of capillary-tunnels (10-15 feet wide)
- Crimson-lit from bioluminescent marrow-moss
- Labyrinthine, easy to get lost
- Natural flowing water from god's lymphatic system

**Function**:
- Black market soul contracts
- Smuggling network
- Information trading
- Entertainment district (bars, pleasure houses, gambling)

**Culture**:
- Neutral ground (no Vigil enforcement)
- Ruled by **The Broker** (mysterious information merchant)
- Code of conduct: violence allowed, but betrayal = exile
- Cairn's bar is social hub here

**Key Establishments**:
1. **The Hollow King's Tavern** (Cairn's bar): Neutral meeting ground, information hub, actually serves good food
2. **Whisper Dens**: Information brokers operate from these
3. **Grace Dens**: Narcotic use tolerated, operated by sub-contractors of Book 2's Maestro

#### The Spine District

**Physical Description**:
- Vertebrae converted into administrative buildings
- Connected by bone-bridges and nerve-cable elevators
- Clean, well-lit, sterile compared to other districts
- Each vertebra houses different government function

**Function**:
- **Tribunal Halls**: Where Lysa works, auditing and contract enforcement
- **Public Accounting Squares**: Verdicts announced, executions performed
- **Contract Registration**: All legal soul contracts recorded here
- **Vigil Administration**: Redemption Mark tracking, assignment coordination

**Culture**:
- Bureaucratic, formal, everyone wears professional robes
- Heavily monitored (security glyphs on every wall)
- Mortals dominate (monsters are workers/subjects, not colleagues)
- Corruption disguised as efficiency

**Lysa's Office**:
- Small chamber in mid-spine
- Walls carved with legal precedents
- Desk covered in case files
- Window overlooking Ribcage (reminder of who she serves)

## MAGIC SYSTEM (BOOK-SPECIFIC APPLICATIONS)

### Soul Contracts in Book 1

**Types Featured**:
1. **Temple Binding**: Cyran's original contract (sexual servitude disguised as holy duty)
2. **Redemption Contracts**: Vigil members' oaths (exploitative terms hidden in complexity)
3. **Intimacy Clauses**: Forbid emotional bonds between certain classes
4. **Blood-Key Contracts**: Grant access to restricted areas (like Heart-Chamber)

**Mechanics Explored**:
- **Contract Scope Creep**: "Serve the temple" interpreted to mean "obey all commands"
- **Consent Violations**: Contracts signed under duress are technically void (if proven)
- **Founder Clauses**: Pre-existing law that predates Contract Lords (Lysa's loophole)
- **Death Clause**: If contracting party dies, contract becomes void (Varos's vulnerability)

**Key Plot Point**:
Lysa discovers Cyran's temple contract was signed when he was dead (technically), making it legally void. This becomes the legal basis for freeing him.

### Redemption Marks

**Visual**: Sigils carved into skin/bone, glow silver when recognized by officials  
**Mechanics**: Each "good deed" earns a mark; 100 marks = legal personhood (supposedly)  
**Reality**: System is rigged—marks can be revoked arbitrarily, personhood never actually granted  
**Cyran's Count**: 87 marks at book start, believes he's close to freedom

**Reveal**: In Ch. 12, Lysa discovers no one has *ever* earned full personhood. The system is designed to exploit indefinitely.

### First Blood Ritual (Book 1 Specific)

**Function**: Vampiric feeding with explicit consent negotiation  
**Requirements**:
- Verbal consent required before each feeding
- Donor can revoke consent mid-feeding (fangs must withdraw)
- Blood-bond risk: repeated feeding from same person creates emotional link

**Lysa & Cyran's Feeding Scenes**:
1. **Ch. 8 (First Blood)**: Emergency medical feeding, consent negotiation under pressure
2. **Ch. 11**: Voluntary feeding, Lysa offers comfort after his trauma episode
3. **Ch. 17**: Intimate feeding, explicitly non-transactional, desire-driven

**Magic Effect**: Creates empathic link, allows them to sense each other's emotions (becomes plot point in climax)
