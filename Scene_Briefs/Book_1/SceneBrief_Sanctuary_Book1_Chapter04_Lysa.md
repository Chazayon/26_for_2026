#### **Plot & Structure**

**Plot Summary:**  
Lysa arrives at Cyran's quarters for the interview. The space is opulent—silk, candlelight, wine waiting; a sharp contrast to the ribcage streets. Cyran is performing: the perfect host, the seductive monster. He offers himself without saying so directly—his body as a bribe for favorable marks. Lysa is tempted (he's beautiful, and she's human) but horrified by the transaction. She declines as currency without humiliating him: *"Your body isn't payment I'll accept."* He's thrown—genuinely confused. This isn't how it works. She redirects to the actual interview; he answers with half-truths. She notes that he expected exploitation—telling her more than the questions did.

**Scene Goal:**  
Lysa: Conduct a professional audit interview.  
Cyran (Antagonist in scene): Ensure safety through seduction/transaction.

**Scene Outcome:**  
The transactional dynamic is shattered. Lysa establishes herself as a moral anomaly in his world.

**Scene Question:**  
Will Lysa succumb to the temptation, or will she maintain her ethical line?

**Ticking Clock/Deadline:**  
Late night fatigue; the desire to leave this dangerous intimacy.

**Opening Hook - First Line Strategy:**


1. *I expected a barracks. I walked into a boudoir.*
2. *He pours the wine like it's a magic trick, watching my throat as I swallow.*
3. *His quarters are a lie wrapped in velvet—too soft for a soldier, too cold for a home.*

#### **Action Beats (25 Minimum)**

1. Lysa stands at the door; Cyran unlocks it with a heavy iron key.
2. She enters. Senses assaulted by luxury: heavy drapes, musk, warmth.
3. She notes the contrast: bone-walls covered in tapestries. Hiding the structure.
4. Cyran moves to a sideboard. "Red or white? The red is... full-bodied." (Innuendo).
5. "Water," she says, sitting at the only hard-backed chair (creating distance).
6. He pours wine for himself, water for her. Brings them over.
7. He doesn't sit. He leans against the table, invading her eyeline.
8. "So, Auditor. What does the file say I am?"
9. She opens her notebook. "It says you're effective. And expensive."
10. He laughs, tracing the rim of his glass. "Quality always is."
11. He moves behind her chair. "I can be... very accommodating to my auditors."
12. Her spine stiffens. She smells him—leather and spice.
13. His hand brushes her shoulder—light, testing.
14. "Most find the report writing easier after... relaxation."
15. Beat of silence. She understands. He's offering the trade.
16. She closes the notebook. Hard.
17. She stands up, facing him. Too close.
18. She sees the calculation in his eyes. It's not desire; it's commerce.
19. "Cyran," she says. Name, not title.
20. "Your body isn't payment I'll accept."
21. He freezes. The glass pauses halfway to his mouth.
22. His expression falters—confusion, then a mask of indifference.
23. "Pity. I'm told I'm excellent payment."
24. "I'm sure you are. But I'm not a merchant."
25. She sits back down. "Now. The deaths on Sector 4. Explain."
26. He stares at her for a long beat. Reassessing threat level.
27. He sits opposite her (finally taking a seat).
28. They talk. He lies, but less performatively.
29. She notes his hands—no longer posing, just gripping the glass.
30. Interview concludes. She packs up.
31. "Same time tomorrow?" he asks, tone sharp/mocking.
32. "Tomorrow we walk the patrol," she says. "Wear something practical."
33. She leaves.
34. Door closes. She exhales the breath she held for an hour.

*Note: The tension comes from the mismatch of expectations.*
