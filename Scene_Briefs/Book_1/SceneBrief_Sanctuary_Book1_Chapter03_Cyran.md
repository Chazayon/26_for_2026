#### **Plot & Structure**

**Plot Summary:**  
Cyran protects his beat near a forbidden blood cistern. He catches an intruder—a figure in dark robes poking around the restricted zone. He has her by the throat/knife-point before he realizes she's not a thief. It's Lysa, investigating independently. When he sees her credentials, he shifts from threat to charm in a heartbeat. Banter ensues: she's unimpressed by his theatrics; he's intrigued by her nerve. He gives her a "tour" of the cistern (arrogant, showing off). She asks direct questions about the deaths. The scene ends with him inviting her to "a proper interview" at his quarters, intending to charm his way out of the audit.

**Scene Goal:**  
Cyran: Neutralize the intruder, then neutralize the *auditor* (via charm).

**Scene Outcome:**  
Cyran realizes Lysa is immune to his usual intimidation/seduction tactics, sparking his interest/frustration.

**Scene Question:**  
Can Cyran control the narrative of this audit, or is Lysa already seeing through him?

**Ticking Clock/Deadline:**  
Patrol isn't over; other threats could appear. Also, the awkwardness of holding a government official at knifepoint.

**Opening Hook - First Line Strategy:**

1. *I smell the ink before I smell the fear—which is rare in my line of work.*
2. *There are three ways to catch a spy in the Ribcage, and my favorite involves gravity.*
3. *Most people beg when they feel the blade. She just sighs.*

#### **Action Beats (25 Minimum)**

1. Cyran is shadowing a figure moving near the cistern edge.
2. The figure (Lysa) pauses to examine a rune on the wall.
3. Cyran drops from the shadows—silent, lethal impactful landing.
4. He grabs her shoulder, spins her; blade to her carotid instantly.
5. "Lost, little shadow?" he whispers, leaning close.
6. She doesn't scream. She stiffens, then holds up a badge.
7. The badge glints in the red cistern light: *Tribunal Inquiry.*
8. Cyran pauses, blinks. The predator mask slips for a micro-second.
9. He steps back, sheathing the dagger with a flourish. "My mistake. You didn't look like a bureaucrat."
10. She adjusts her robes, annoyed. "And you don't look like a protector."
11. He laughs—theatrical, sharp. "I'm the Blood Relic, darling. I look exactly like necessity."
12. She ignores the line. "I'm Auditor Voss. I'm reviewing your patrol."
13. He circles her—invading personal space. Testing her reaction.
14. She stands her ground, eyes tracking him. Not prey behavior.
15. He gestures to the cistern. "Here for the view? It's lovely this time of night. If you like gore."
16. She walks *closer* to the edge. "I'm here for the evidence. Three bodies found here last week."
17. He joins her. "God-spawn. Messy eaters." (A lie/deflection).
18. She looks at him, searching his face. "The reports said the wounds were... precise."
19. He leans on a bone-pillar, posing. "I'm a precise killer. It's why they pay me."
20. She takes a note in a small book. "Arrogance noted."
21. He bristles—she's writing? "Is 'charming' on that list?"
22. "No." She closes the book.
23. He changes tactics—softens the voice, lowers the lids. The Seducer.
24. "This isn't the place for official business. My quarters are nearby. Wine? Fire?"
25. She considers it. "An interview. Yes. wine... optional."
26. He offers an arm. She ignores it and walks past him.
27. He watches her go, unsettled.
28. He touches his own neck where he imagines a collar might be.
29. He follows.

*Note: The physical choreography acts out the "Push/Pull" of their dynamic.*

#### **Emotional Architecture**

**Emotional Beats (5-8 Key Moments):**

1. **Predatory Focus:** Hunting the intruder. Control.
2. **Surprise:** The badge. Loss of control.
3. **Recovery/Performance:** Putting the mask back on. Charm as defense.
4. **Annoyance:** She's not impressed. She's actually bored/critical.
5. **Defensiveness:** Her questions touch on the truth (the deaths/Varos). He lies to protect himself.
6. **Intrigue:** She didn't flinch. That's new.
7. **Calculated Charm:** The invite. He thinks he can "handle" her in his territory.
8. **Uncertainty:** She walked past him. He's not the one leading this dance.

**Character Arc Moment:**  
Cyran tries his two main modes (Violence and Seduction) and both fail to rattle Lysa. He has to improvise.

**Relationship Dynamics:**  
**Cyran & Lysa:** Instantly established as equals. He has physical power; she has institutional/intellectual power.

**Subtext Layer:**  
He's terrified she'll find the truth (the temple magic). She's terrified she's safer with a monster than with the Tribunal. Both are pretending to be colder than they are.
