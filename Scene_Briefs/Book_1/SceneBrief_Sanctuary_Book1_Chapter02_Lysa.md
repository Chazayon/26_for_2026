#### **Plot & Structure**

**Plot Summary:**  
Lysa is in the Spine District's Tribunal halls, watching a "public accounting" where a monster's Merit Marks are being revoked. The creature screams as sigils burn away its legal personhood. Her superior pulls her aside for a special assignment in the ribcage district. A high-ranking Vigil protector—the infamous "Blood Relic"—has had complaints filed (unexplained deaths, improper feeding). Her job: audit his record, observe his conduct, recommend action. She reads Cyran's sanitized file—filled with gaps and euphemisms—and expects a monster gaming the system.

**Scene Goal:**  
To receive her new assignment and understand the stakes of the "Blood Relic" case.

**Scene Outcome:**  
Lysa accepts the assignment with skepticism, setting her on a collision course with Cyran.

**Scene Question:**  
Is the "Blood Relic" a monster gaming the system, or something else entirely?

**Ticking Clock/Deadline:**  
The complaints are escalating; the Tribunal wants this resolved *quietly* and *quickly* before donor relations sour.

**Opening Hook - First Line Strategy:**


1. *I have audited three hundred monsters, and every single one of them claimed to be innocent.*
2. *The Spine District smells of ink and judgment, and today, judgment is loud.*
3. *The sound of a soul being legally dismantled is surprisingly dry—like tearing parchment.*

#### **Action Beats (25 Minimum)**

1. Lysa stands in the observation gallery of the Tribunal Hall.
2. Below, a Magistrate reads a list of infractions to a cowering ghoul-kin.
3. The Magistrate raises a rod; the ghoul's skin smokes as a Mark vanishes.
4. Lysa flinches—micro-movement, adjusting her spectacles to hide it.
5. She makes a precise note in her ledger: *Procedure 77-B administered. Subject distress: High.*
6. A clerk taps her shoulder: "High Inquisitor Voss wants you."
7. She closes the ledger with a sharp snap and follows.
8. Walking the halls: passing endless rows of scribes and dusty bone-shelves.
9. She notes a cracked tile—the infrastructure is failing, even here.
10. Arrival at Voss's office: mahogany desk, view of the ribcage, smell of expensive tea.
11. Voss doesn't look up immediately (power move).
12. He slides a file across the desk: *Subject: Cyran, designation Blood Relic.*
13. Lysa picks it up; it's heavy, bound in red leather.
14. Voss outlines the complaints: dead civilians, missing time, "unbecoming conduct."
15. She opens the file: finding redacting ink, vague terms like "temple rites."
16. "You want me to audit the city's poster boy?" she asks.
17. Voss nods. "We need to know if he's broken. Or if he's... hungry."
18. She skims a page: *Merit Marks earned: 5,000. Status: Indentured.*
19. She spots a Contract Lord's seal on the complaint: *Thessen.*
20. Suspicion prickles—Thessen is powerful. Why does he care?
21. She accepts the task: "I'll be thorough."
22. "Be quick," Voss corrects.
23. She leaves the office, clutching the file like a weapon.
24. Back in the hallway, she watches the ghoul being dragged away, marking gone.
25. She looks at Cyran's name again. *Don't be a monster,* she thinks. *For your sake.*

*Note: The scene establishes the bureaucratic cruelty of the world and Lysa's place within it.*

#### **Emotional Architecture**

**Emotional Beats (5-8 Key Moments):**

1. **Discomfort:** Watching the punishment. She hates the cruelty but respects the law.
2. **Duty:** Being summoned resets her to "professional mode."
3. **Curiosity:** The file is thick, redacted, and intriguing.
4. **Skepticism:** "Blood Relic" sounds like a title for a prima donna, not a protector.
5. **Suspicion:** Thessen's involvement smells wrong.
6. **Determination:** She will find the truth, no matter what Voss wants.
7. **Empathy (stifled):** Seeing the ghoul dragged away reminds her of what failure looks like.
8. **Resolve:** She enters "hunter mode"—seeking truth.

**Character Arc Moment:**  
Lysa questions the "sanitized" file, showing she values truth over the official narrative—the trait that will save Cyran.

**Relationship Dynamics:**  
**Lysa vs. Voss:** Subordinate but intellectually superior. She sees the game; he plays it.

**Subtext Layer:**  
The Tribunal is corrupt. Voss doesn't want justice; he wants a clean PR problem. Lysa knows this but hopes to do good anyway.

####  **World Interaction & Secondary Characters**

**Secondary Character Interactions:**

- **Auditor Kess (Colleague):** Junior auditor in Lysa's department, mid-20s, always has ink-stained fingers and a nervous energy. Approaches Lysa's desk during the file review scene, ostensibly to borrow a reference ledger but actually to gossip about Cyran's file.
  - **Distinct voice/mannerism:** Speaks in excited half-whispers, uses bureaucratic euphemisms ("compliance irregularities" instead of "murders"), fidgets with her quill

**Dialogue Moments:**

- **Worldbuilding:** Kess mentions "They say he's killed thirty god-spawn this quarter. Thirty! How does a Relic even count that high?" Reveals that A) Relics are viewed as property/weapons, not mathematically competent beings, and B) The kill quotas are tracked/gossiped about
- **Local gossip:** "Did you hear? Someone requested his full creation file. That hasn't been opened since Thessen first commissioned him decades ago." Establishes that Lysa's investigation is already noticed and unusual
- **Speech pattern difference:** Kess uses casual office shorthand ("files," "quotas," "the Vigil brass") vs. Lysa's more formal, precise legal terminology

**Character Interaction Beats:**

- **How Lysa treats Kess:** Polite but distracted, doesn't encourage the gossip but doesn't shut it down either. Shows Lysa is weary of office culture but not cruel to colleagues.
- **What it reveals:** Lysa is an outsider even among her peers—she doesn't participate in the dehumanization of Relics that's casual office conversation for others
- **The moment:** When Kess says something particularly callous ("At least if he goes feral, he's pretty to look at"), Lysa stiffens and gives a cold "That will be all, Kess," showing her protective instincts are already forming

**Environmental Engagement:**

- **Background activity:** Other auditors shuffling papers, the scratch of quills on parchment, a clerk delivering message tubes via pneumatic system (whoosh and thunk), the distant toll of the Tribunal clock marking the hour
-** Objects touched:** Lysa runs her finger down the ledger index, stamps a requisition form with her official seal (the wax is still warm), adjusts the oil lamp on her desk when the light flickers
- **Physical interaction:** She notices someone left a half-eaten apple on the file cart (shows the mundane reality of bureaucracy), straightens a stack of case files that's leaning precariously

**Purpose:**
Kess's gossip prevents the file-reading scene from being pure exposition dumps. The dialogue shows how Relics are discussed by "normal" people, establishes the surveillance Lysa is already under, and gives Lysa a chance to demonstrate her character (protective, principled) through how she responds to casual cruelty. The environmental details ground the scene in office reality—it's not a dramatic noir detective office, it's a boring bureaucracy with squeaky file carts and half-eaten fruit.
